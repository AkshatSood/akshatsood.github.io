Lambda Corps

This is the Second Assignment for Programming Practice and Application (PPA). The game is a text based adventure game called ‘Lambda Corps’. It has various rooms which the player can go through, characters and items to interact with and objectives to complete. The game has been inspired from multiple sources. Some of the more important inspirations for the game are ‘The Stanley Parable’ and ‘Wolfenstein 3D’.

The game aims to create an interactive and interesting environment for the user. In addition to the many commands the user can use and the special features of the game, I have also incorporated numerous gaming and music references throughout the game.

The Game ‘Lambda Corps’ takes place in the future, on an Earth in which World War 3 has already taken place. World War 4 is going on, and the 2 sides, the United Federation and the Alliance, are using Cyber Warfare to fight. Due to this changed way of warfare, huge software companies have been added to the army. The protagonist’s name is William Joseph Blazkowicz, and he has just started working at one of these companies called Lambda Corps.

During the game, you can move through rooms, pick up or drop items and talk to different characters in the game. You are given multiple tasks throughout the game. The next task dialogue is only displayed once you have completed the current task. At any given point, if you forget the task, entering the ‘task’ command will tell you what the current task is. In case you get lost, then you can use the ‘help’ command to print the list of valid commands and the map of the facility, which also shows your current location.

As the game progresses, so do the days passed since you started playing. This gives the game a sense of progression. Certain tasks will make the game skip a few days, and so will going to the Recreational Room. However there is a limit to the number of days you can proceed to. Once the game reaches 75 days, you automatically lose the game and one of the possible outcomes is printed.

In the game you meet another employee in the facility, called Stanley. He tells you that the Chief of the Corps might be betraying the United Federation and leaking secrets to the Alliance. You are required to help him and perform the required tasks. You help him find proof of the Chief’s treason, or so he says. To stop the chief from leaking the code for the ‘Reboot Matrix’ of the United Federation Stanley has planted a bug in the Chief’s ‘Superluminal Transmitter’. Unfortunately because of his activities, Stanley has been banned from the facility. Now it is up to the player to activate the bug in the transmitter.

Once you reach the Chief’s office, you get to know that the Chief thinks that Stanley is the mole. The Chief tells you that Stanley has always been suspicious and now has turned out to be a spy for the Alliance who has already leaked information to them. Because of your interactions with him, suspicion has fallen on you as well. The Chief asks you to prove your loyalty by fixing the bug in the superluminal transmitter, so that he can send a message to the Headquarters. This is when the player is faced with a choice. You can either ‘activate’ the bug or you can ‘fix’ it. As it turns out Stanley was the mole along. When you fix the bug, you end up saving the United Federation and winning the game! If you choose to activate the bug, you end up losing the game. Other than this there are a few secret endings to the game. Once you have collected the CSB from the Server Room, you can actually go to the Chief and give it to him instead of giving it to Stanley. This will end the game as well and you will win since you were able to stop the attack. Also if you pick the rose found outside the facility and give it to Emily, then if you win the game, Emily asks you out on a date.

The Bo"Real" Forest

For this assignment, we have chosen to simulate the Canadian ecosystem, closely resembling that of the Boreal forest. Animals like bears, wolves, elks, moose, wolverines and salmon populate the landscape along with 2 species of plants. Environmental factors like day and night, seasons, weather and terrain have also been simulated. The simulation contains a total of 3 different zones; the land, the shore and the lake. Bears, elks, moose, wolves and wolverines are land animals and are thus only allowed in the shore and the land. On the other hand Salmon are allowed in the lake and shore and cannot go to the land.

The animals can be carnivores, herbivores or omnivores. This determines what each animal will be able to eat. Each animal has a breeding season and if they are hibernating animals, then a hibernating season as well. The plants grow differently in different seasons and their growth is affected by the weather.

AirBnb Themed Property Marketplace

This is an app created to explore available properties inside London using the property dataset of Airbnb. It’s designed to be easy to use, and powerful in the data it can display. Creating the piece of software used all the unique talents of Team Hummus, and we split the work evenly between us through GitHub and peer programming at Bush House.

The GUI carries a modern design with “matte” black backgrounds, bright pink elements and minimalism. Since the application is designed to be for Airbnb, the oﬃcial “Airbnb pink” was used for text and borders to adhere to company design standards. The base idea of the GUI is to disguise the complexity of the backend behind a deceptively simple user interface.
